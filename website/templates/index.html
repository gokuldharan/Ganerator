Description

caterator.me is a neural net which is trained to produce images of cats using generative adversarial networks (GAN). Despite the abstract term, the principle is concrete: Network A is trained to recognize cats, while Network B is trained to create pictures that are recognized as cats. After enough training repetitions, Network A is "tricked" into thinking that the images produced by Network B are real-life photographs of cats. If the networks are well enough trained, it is likely that people will be tricked too.
Detailed explanation of the subject matter is found here.

Instructions

To prouce an image, click on the "Generate" button and the image will be loaded in the center tray. You can see for yourself how real the cats look!

Other Examples

There have been numerous experiments on GAN, including faces and hotel bedrooms. Some of them come very close to resembling real images, however this is limited at higher resolutions.
